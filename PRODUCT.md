# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is recruiters, engineering managers, and potential clients looking for a frontend or full-stack developer. They need to understand Abdelrahman Mohamed's capabilities quickly, examine credible evidence in completed work, and decide whether to begin a hiring or project conversation.

## Product Purpose

This responsive Next.js portfolio presents Abdelrahman's skills and completed projects for desktop and mobile browser visitors. Its primary goal is to lead to an interview for a frontend or full-stack developer position. Its secondary goal is to encourage direct contact from recruiters, engineering managers, or potential freelance clients. A successful visit makes the work easy to understand, demonstrates quality through evidence, and gives visitors confidence to make contact.

## Positioning

Abdelrahman combines engineering discipline with close attention to user experience, turning ideas into polished, responsive, accessible, and maintainable web experiences rather than stopping when the code merely works.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: “View my work,” linking directly to the projects section.
- Secondary CTA: “Download résumé.”
- Contact path: A clearly visible “Contact me” action in the navigation and again after the projects section.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: “I build polished, accessible web experiences that combine thoughtful user experience with reliable, maintainable engineering.”
- Belief ladder: Visitors first understand that Abdelrahman thinks carefully about users and real problems. They then see that he can translate those needs into polished frontend experiences and reliable full-stack implementations. The projects provide concrete evidence of technical decisions, attention to detail, and the ability to finish meaningful work. Finally, visitors believe he communicates clearly, cares about quality, and would be dependable and thoughtful to work with.
- Proof on hand: Completed projects, project screenshots, GitHub repositories, source-code quality, and technical decisions that Abdelrahman can explain. Credibility should come from concise case-study descriptions covering the problem, his role, technical approach, challenges, and result. There are currently no verified testimonials, client logos, or measurable business outcomes, and the portfolio must not invent them.

## Brand Personality

Thoughtful, precise, and approachable. The portfolio communicates clearly, demonstrates careful attention to detail, and feels professional without becoming cold, arrogant, or overly corporate. It should leave visitors feeling confidence, trust, and enough curiosity to explore the projects and make contact.

Reference principles include Linear's clarity and disciplined hierarchy, Vercel's typography and technical polish, and Brittany Chiang's clear project storytelling and restrained interaction. These are principles to interpret personally, not visual styles to copy.

## Anti-references

The portfolio must not resemble a generic template or AI-generated landing page. Avoid excessive purple or blue gradients, glowing backgrounds, pervasive glassmorphism, too many rounded cards, cards nested inside cards, oversized empty hero sections, skill-percentage bars, decorative technology-logo clouds, excessive animation, typewriter effects, vague claims, and copied startup-style language. It should not feel cluttered, gimmicky, overly corporate, or visually impressive at the expense of readability and project evidence.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with evidence: Make completed work and explainable decisions more persuasive than broad claims.
2. Join UX with engineering: Show that thoughtful user experience and reliable implementation are parts of the same standard of quality.
3. Make the path obvious: Help time-constrained visitors understand the work, inspect proof, and make contact without friction.
4. Communicate with precision: Use direct, human language and clear hierarchy without inflated startup language or invented outcomes.
5. Earn every flourish: Let interaction and visual polish support readability, project storytelling, and trust.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA is the minimum standard. The portfolio must support complete keyboard navigation, visible focus indicators, semantic HTML, descriptive link and button labels, appropriate heading structure, useful alternative text, screen-reader-friendly organization, sufficient contrast, and communication that does not depend on color alone. Touch targets must be large enough, text must resize responsively, reduced-motion preferences must be respected, and no interaction may depend only on hover, animation, or color.
